## Impact on PM Work Areas

### Design Collaboration (20-30% of PM Time)
- **Current AI Capability:** 10-20%
- **What Works:** Design description, element identification
- **What Doesn't:** Strategic feedback, design intent, UX quality assessment
- **Gap:** 50-70% potential vs. 10-20% current

### User Testing Analysis (10-15% of PM Time)
- **Current AI Capability:** 5-10%
- **What Works:** Video transcription, action description
- **What Doesn't:** Behavior interpretation, pain points, emotions, usability issues
- **Gap:** 50-70% potential vs. 5-10% current

### Competitive Analysis (5-10% of PM Time)
- **Current AI Capability:** 10-15%
- **What Works:** Competitor design identification, UI description
- **What Doesn't:** Strategic analysis, differentiation, market trends
- **Gap:** 40-50% potential vs. 10-15% current

### Prototype Evaluation (10-15% of PM Time)
- **Current AI Capability:** 5-10%
- **What Works:** Static screenshot analysis
- **What Doesn't:** Interactive prototypes, flows, interactions, state changes
- **Gap:** 40-50% potential vs. 5-10% current

## Bottom Line

**Current State:**
- GenAI multi-modal capabilities: **5-20% of PM visual/design work**
- Biggest gaps: Strategic understanding, interactive prototypes, behavior interpretation
- Impact: **Limited assistance** with design and video work

**Key Insight:**
Multi-modal GenAI is **5-10 years behind text capabilities** for PM work. PMs should:
- Use AI for **description and transcription** today
- Keep humans for **strategic design and video analysis**
- Wait for **fundamental breakthroughs** (2-3 years) for transformative impact

**Timeline:**
- **6-12 months:** Basic improvements (better descriptions, transcription)
- **1-2 years:** Medium-term advances (temporal understanding, context integration)
- **2-3 years:** Fundamental breakthroughs (strategic understanding, interactive prototypes, behavior interpretation)
